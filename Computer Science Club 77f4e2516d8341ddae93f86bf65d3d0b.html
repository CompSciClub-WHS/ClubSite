FAQs

Do I need to know how to code to join?

Nope. We are willing to teach to anyone who is interested in computer science. Obviously, having a background in computer science, math, or programming helps, but it's not necessary to get started.

If you are or have taken either programming or AP Computer Science at the high school, we would encourage you to join the club. It's a chance to apply the skills you've been taught in class and to expand your knowledge and understanding of computer science.

When and where do we meet?

The club will meet during activity blocks. We are also currently considering organizing additional meeting times during the week. We do not have a room to meet in yet. That information will be posted as soon as we have it confirmed.

How big the commitment to CS club?

The commitment is as great or as little as you want it to be. Anyone can attend any number of meetings or activities we offer. We obviously recommend you come to all of our meetings and discussions, but none are mandatory.

Are there tryouts to take part in competitions?

The main competition we are taking part in is the American Computer Science League (ACSL). Historically, all of our members have been able to attend competitions because our club simply hasn't been big enough to fill all of the open spots on the team. If that changes, it is possible that we will create a tryout system to determine who gets to be on the competition team.
